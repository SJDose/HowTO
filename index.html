How to Create a Paper Art Picture

What is it?

The type of paper art I'll be teaching you how to make is one that I have used multiple times.
It invloves cutting out the shadows of an image and putting that over a watercolor background.

What You'll Need

The supplies needed are fairly flexible for this project, so feel free to substitute the ones listed here for more readily availabe supplies

  • Thick white paper of desired size
  • Water color paper of the same size
  • Water color paints
  • Soft bristle brushes (the more absorbment the better)
  • Exacto knife
  • Glue
  • Reference photo (in grayscale)
  • Sharpie
  • Pencil
  • Transparency sheet or tracing paper (optional)

The Process

Time to start working!

Preperation

To start you'll need to simplify your reference photo. You'll do this by drawing on top of it with your sharpie.
If you want to preserve the photo just tape the transparency or tracing paperover the top of the photo and draw on that instead.
Draw over the image with simple straight lines, you can be as detailed as you want. Then you need to outline and fill in the shadows you'll be cutting out.
Try to make sure that none of your shadows or lines go of the page, otherwise when you cut them out you'll end up with a bunch of puzzle pieces.
Another step you might want to do is to draw a grid over your picture. This will make keeping you picture proportional easier.

The Main Drawing

If you want to you can create the background before or during this step.

Take your thick paper (not the water color paper!) and start drawing. If you created a grid on your reference photo then make another one, proportional to your first one, on your larger paper.
Then copy the lines you made on your picture onto your paper. Use your pencil and draw lightly so you can easily erase the lines.

Now you're ready to cut! Carefully use your exacto nknife to cut out the shadows.
If there are lines that you want to be seen, but are not a part of a shadow then cut out a small rectangle where those lines are.
Try and make sure different lines don't touch or your paper could turn into a mess.

Background

Take your water color paper and paint it.
It's that easy. If you want to you can paint a picture or the colors of your drawing.
Or you could just randomly paint.

Put It Together

Now just glue your drawing to the background and you have your masterpeice!
